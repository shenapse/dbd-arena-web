**Structured Dead by Daylight competition, built to be repeated.**

DBD Arena organizes rated custom matches for killers and survivor teams. Play
under published rules, face opponents selected using relevant Arena ratings,
and build evidence of your performance across matches.

Each match stands on its own. You can compete seriously without entering a
tournament bracket or committing to a complete event.

## Competition built for repeated play

Public matches can be played repeatedly, but they are not organized around
fixed competitive conditions. Tournaments provide strict rules and direct
competition, but each event is bounded.

Arena provides an unique competitive format: repeatable like public matches, structured like tournaments, but not bound to either model.
DBD Arena uses published rules and restrictions in standalone matches that can
be played again and again. Arena also uses competitive records to look for opponents near your
current level, so each result adds useful evidence about your performance over
time.

## Choose how you compete

### Queue as killer

Join alone and choose the killer you want to play. Arena looks for a survivor
team using your record with that killer.
Your results are tied to the killer you chose instead of being mixed
with your results on other killers.
Your results then show how you perform on that killer, under the same competitive format, across repeated matches.

### Queue as a survivor team

Join the queue with other survivors and face a killer selected using the
relevant Arena records. Your team gets serious, repeatable matches under the
same published competitive format each time, with known rules. Over time, those results build a cleaner
record of how that survivor team performs.

## Choose a Format to Queue

Arena matches are organized by competitive format. A format is the published
ruleset for a queue: it defines who can enter, which killers or survivor teams
can play, what maps and restrictions apply, how the match is scored, and which
ratings the result affects.

Check the [available Arena formats](/game-modes/) to see what you can queue
for now. Each format page explains its match conditions before you enter the
queue.

For most new players, **DBD 1v4 Quartet Ranked** is the recommended starting
point. It is Arena’s standard rated killer-versus-survivor-team format, but it
is one example of the formats Arena can support.

## How an Arena match works

The live service coordinates matches in Discord. The match itself is played as
a normal custom game, under the selected format’s published
rules.

1. **Join the queue.** Queue alone as a killer or together as a survivor team.
2. **Receive the match details.** Arena provides the opponent, killer information, map
   selection, rules, and win conditions before play begins.
3. **Play the custom match.** Both sides play a custom match using the
   agreed format conditions.
4. **Report the result.** players report the match result. Once accepted, it is recorded and the relevant ratings are updated.

Arena coordinates the competition; it does not replace the game itself.
