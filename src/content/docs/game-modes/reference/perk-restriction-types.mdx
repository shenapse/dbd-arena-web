Some balancing pages include perk restrictions that go beyond a simple allowed
or banned list. A perk can be allowed on its own, but still become illegal when
it is paired with another perk or repeated by another player.

This page explains the two most common restriction types: **repetition limits** and **combo bans**.

## Allowed Perks vs. Restricted Builds

An allowed perk is not always the same thing as an allowed build.

For example, on a balancing page, both `Call of Brine` and `Eruption`
may appear as allowed killer perks. That means each perk can be used by itself.
However, if the page also says that `Call of Brine + Eruption` is a banned
combination, the killer may not use those two perks together in a build.

The same idea applies to survivor perks. `Decisive Strike` may appear as an
allowed survivor perk, but if it is also subject to a team-level repetition
limit, only the allowed number of survivors may bring it in the same match.

## Repetition Limit

A repetition limit controls how many times the same perk may appear within the
rule's scope.

### Example: Duo+Duo game mode

In Duo+Duo, survivor repetition limits often apply inside each duo. If a page
says that duo partners may not repeat survivor perks, then the two players in
the same duo cannot bring the same perk.

Suppose repetition limits apply to each duo, and they apply to **all perks**.
The survivor teams are Duo 1, with Player A and Player B, and Duo 2, with
Player C and Player D.

- Legal: Player A brings `Iron Will`, and Player B brings `Dead Hard`
- Legal: Player A brings `Sprint Burst`, and Player B brings `Fixated`
- Illegal: Player A brings `Lithe`, and Player B also brings `Lithe`

![Illustration: duo repetition limit (no repeated perks within a duo)](/images/builds/reference/perk-restriction-types/duo-repetition-limit/The-Trapper-survivor-perks.png)

In this case, your build's legality depends on your duo partner's build. It does
not depend on the other duo's builds.

## Combo Ban

A combo ban means that specific perks cannot be used together.

**Example:**

Suppose `Call of Brine` and `Eruption` are listed as allowed perks, and the
same page also names them as a combo ban.

- Legal: a build with `Call of Brine`, but not `Eruption`
- Legal: a build with `Eruption`, but not `Call of Brine`
- Illegal: a build with both `Call of Brine` and `Eruption`

![Illustration: killer combo ban (Call of Brine + Eruption)](/images/builds/reference/perk-restriction-types/killer-combo-ban/The-Trapper-killer-perks.png)

Combo bans can apply to one player's own build or to a group of players,
depending on how the rule is written on that balancing page.

For killer perks, this is straightforward: a combo ban always applies to the
killer's own build. For survivor perks, it may apply to one survivor's build or
to the builds of multiple teammates, depending on the restriction's *scope*.

## Rule Scope

The scope of a restriction tells you where the rule is checked.

For killers, the scope is always the killer's own build. For survivors, the
scope may be one individual survivor, one duo, or the whole survivor team.

Scope changes what other builds you need to compare against. For a repetition
limit, the same perk is counted only within that scope. For a combo ban, the
banned perks are checked together only within that scope.

That means a survivor restriction with an individual scope only checks one
survivor's own build. A duo-scoped restriction checks the two builds in that duo
together. A whole-team restriction checks all survivor builds together.

For Duo+Duo survivor rules, a restriction may apply to your own duo rather than
to all four survivors. In that case, you only need to compare your build with your
duo partner's build. The other survivor duo follows the same rule within their
own duo.

The examples below show how the same restriction type changes depending on its
scope.

## Examples

### Example 1: Combo Ban for Killer Build

- Scope: Killer
- Restriction type: Combo ban

This works like the `Call of Brine + Eruption` example above. At the time of
writing, this is the only restriction pattern beyond the allowed perk list that
killers need to check.

### Example 2: Combo Ban for One Survivor

- Scope: One survivor
- Restriction type: Combo ban

This works the same way as a killer combo ban, but it applies to one survivor's
own build.

Suppose `Deliverance` and `Unbreakable` are listed as allowed survivor perks,
and the same page also names them as a combo ban for one survivor.

- Legal: A build with `Deliverance`, but not `Unbreakable`
- Legal: A build with `Unbreakable`, but not `Deliverance`
- Illegal: A build with both `Unbreakable` and `Deliverance`

![Illustration: combo ban for one survivor (Deliverance + Unbreakable)](/images/builds/reference/perk-restriction-types/survivor-combo-ban/The-Trapper-survivor-perks.png)

### Example 3: Combo Ban for Duo

- Scope: Duo
- Restriction type: Combo ban

Suppose a 1v4 Duo+Duo balancing page lists `Sprint Burst` and
`Decisive Strike` as allowed perks, and also names them as a combo ban for each
duo.

- Legal: You bring `Sprint Burst`, and your duo partner uses a build **without** `Decisive Strike`
- Illegal: You bring `Sprint Burst`, and your duo partner uses a build **with** `Decisive Strike`
- Illegal: You bring both `Sprint Burst` and `Decisive Strike`
- Legal: You bring `Decisive Strike`, and a survivor outside your duo brings `Sprint Burst`

![Illustration: combo ban for a duo (Sprint Burst + Decisive Strike)](/images/builds/reference/perk-restriction-types/duo-combo-ban/The-Trapper-survivor-perks.png)

### Example 4: Repetition Limits for Duo

This works like the Duo+Duo example in the **Repetition Limit** section above.
If the restriction applies to each duo, the same perk cannot appear more than
once within your duo.

### Example 5: Combo Ban for Whole Survivor Team

- Scope: Whole survivor team
- Restriction type: Combo ban

Suppose a 1v4 Quartet balancing page lists `Sprint Burst` and
`Decisive Strike` as allowed perks, and also names them as a combo ban for the
whole survivor team.

In this case, the restriction is checked across all four survivors.

- Legal: You bring `Sprint Burst`, and no survivor brings `Decisive Strike`
- Legal: You bring `Decisive Strike`, and no survivor brings `Sprint Burst`
- Illegal: You bring `Sprint Burst`, and one of your teammates brings `Decisive Strike`
- Illegal: You bring both `Sprint Burst` and `Decisive Strike`

![Illustration: combo ban for the whole survivor team (Sprint Burst + Decisive Strike)](/images/builds/reference/perk-restriction-types/team-combo-ban/The-Trapper-survivor-perks.png)

### Example 6: Repetition Limit for Whole Survivor Team

- Scope: Whole survivor team
- Restriction type: Repetition limit

Suppose a 1v4 Quartet balancing page says that survivor perks may not be
repeated across the whole survivor team.

In this case, the same perk cannot appear more than once among all four
survivors.

- Legal: Player A brings `Iron Will`, Player B brings `Dead Hard`, Player C brings `Sprint Burst`, and Player D brings `Bond`
- Illegal: Player A brings `Lithe`, and Player B also brings `Lithe`
- Illegal: Player A brings `Adrenaline`, and Player C in the other duo also brings `Adrenaline`

![Illustration: repetition limit for the whole survivor team (no repeated perks across all four survivors)](/images/builds/reference/perk-restriction-types/team-repetition-limit/The-Trapper-survivor-perks.png)
