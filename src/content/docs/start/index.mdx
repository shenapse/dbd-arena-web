## Prepare for that path

Each path has a different setup. Do this before you submit a queue request.

| Path | Before queueing |
|---|---|
| 1v1 player | Review the [1v1 Symmetric Ranked regulation](/regulations/1v1-symmetric/), including its general rules and per-killer balancing. You queue solo and declare one available killer. |
| 1v4 killer | Review the [1v4 Quartet Ranked regulation](/regulations/1v4-quartet/), including its general rules and the balancing for the killer you want to play. You queue solo and declare one available killer. |
| 1v4 survivor | Gather a fixed four-player party, make sure every member is registered, create a [group](/handbook/groups/), and review the [1v4 Quartet Ranked regulation](/regulations/1v4-quartet/). |

:::note[Custom matches]
DBD Arena coordinates rated custom matches. You still play the match in Dead by
Daylight, so you need the game and the ability to join custom games.
:::

## Join Discord and set up your profile

<LinkButton href={DISCORD_INVITE} icon="discord" target="_blank">Join Discord</LinkButton>

Your first action in Discord is to register a [player profile](/handbook/player-profile/).
Registration creates the identity Arena uses for matchmaking, results, and
ratings.

When you register, you provide:

- A display name.
- Your Dead by Daylight in-game player id.

Your Discord account is linked automatically. You only register once, and you
cannot queue until registration is complete.

## Submit your first queue request

Once your profile is ready, follow the path you chose.

<Steps>
1. **Open the queue for the regulation you want.**
   Use the Discord interface for either 1v1 Symmetric Ranked or 1v4 Quartet
   Ranked.

2. **Choose solo or group entry.**
   1v1 players and 1v4 killers queue solo. A 1v4 survivor team queues through
   its four-player group.

3. **Make any required declaration.**
   Killer-side entries choose the killer they are committing to for the match.
   The regulation page lists what each format requires.

4. **Submit the request and wait.**
   You can have only one active request at a time. If you want to switch
   formats, cancel the current request first.

5. **Respond to the ready check.**
   When Arena finds a match, every participant must confirm readiness. If anyone
   leaves or times out, the match is cancelled and everyone can queue again.

6. **Play and confirm the result.**
   After everyone is ready, set up the custom game, play under the selected
   regulation, then report and approve the result in Discord.
</Steps>

For the complete flow, see the [Matchmaking Journey](/handbook/matchmaking-journey/).
