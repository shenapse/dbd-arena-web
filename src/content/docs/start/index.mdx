The live service runs in Discord. This website only explains how it works — the
actual playing happens there. Here is what you need, what a match looks like,
and how to get in.

## What you need

A **Discord account**. That is the only prerequisite to read about and join the
service. You will of course also need Dead by Daylight and the ability to play
custom (private) games, since that is where matches happen.

## What happens in a match

So you know what you are getting into, the normal match loop looks like this:

- You join the queue for the active regulation.
- The matchmaker pairs you with eligible opponents.
- A match thread is created. All parties confirm readiness.
- You play the match in-game.
- Both sides submit and agree on the result.
- Ratings are updated automatically.

That is the full loop. The [Handbook](/handbook/how-matchmaking-works/) covers
each step in detail, and [Regulations](/regulations/) lists every format you can
play.

## Join Discord, then register

The live service runs in Discord. This is an external handoff — registering,
queueing, readying up, and reporting results all happen there.

<LinkButton href={DISCORD_INVITE} icon="discord" target="_blank">Join Discord</LinkButton>

**Your first action there is to register.** Registering creates your player
profile: you give a display name and your in-game player id, and the service
links the profile to your Discord account automatically. It is a one-time step,
and you cannot queue until it is done. Full detail is in
[Player Profile](/handbook/player-profile/).

:::note[A reminder before you go]
This is a community service for custom matches. It is not official Dead by
Daylight, and it does not affect your in-game rank or progression.
:::
