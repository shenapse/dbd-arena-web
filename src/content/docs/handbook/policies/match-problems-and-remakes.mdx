What happens when a match cannot be played cleanly from start to finish.

## Ready-up and leaving

- Queueing is a commitment: once a queue is formed, each player must confirm ready within the ready-up window, and failing to do so may result in a warning or penalty.
- After a match starts, players may not leave except under the restart and remake rules below; by choosing to queue, players accept responsibility for staying until the match ends.

<Aside type="caution" title="Respect other players' time">
  If it is clear that you cannot play the formed match for any reason, choose to leave during ready-up instead of confirming ready. Early leaving is better than pretending to be ready and then making delayed leave. 
</Aside>

## Pre-match trouble

If a problem stops the match before it really begins — for example, a player does not show up, the custom game cannot be set up, or someone cannot connect — report the match as **not played** rather than as a win or loss. No rating changes.

<Aside type="caution" title="Honesty matters here">
  Reporting a real loss as "not played" to dodge a rating change is result manipulation, and is handled as a conduct violation.
</Aside>


## Mid-match trouble

### Voluntary remake

If something goes wrong during a match and all participants still agree to play, they may remake the match. A remade match is played and reported as a fresh match.

This is allowed even when one player caused the trouble, as long as every participant freely agrees to give that player another chance and the agreement does not violate [Participation and Conduct](/handbook/policies/participation-and-conduct/). The rules below define the default path when players do not all agree to remake.

### Valid remake requests

Players may ask for a remake whenever they believe a valid remake reason applies.

Evidence must be submitted, or the opponent(s) must agree that the reason is valid. If neither condition is met, opponent(s) may refuse the remake and report the match as **not played**.

Valid reasons include:

- **Regulation violation:** wrong perks, add-ons, or offerings; wrong map; duplicate survivors; or another setup that violates the applicable regulation.
- **Unusually high killer ping or wrong server:** this is treated as game-side trouble, not as trouble caused by a player.
- **Player disconnect caused by internet trouble or a game error.**

Players must not abuse remake requests. A player may be sanctioned if they intentionally violate a rule to restart a match.

### Mandatory early restart window

If a valid remake request applies during either of these early windows, players must restart the match even without full agreement:

- Within 30 seconds after the match starts.
- Within 30 seconds after the first survivor is downed, before the second generator is completed.

### Effect of a remake request

Outside the mandatory early restart window, remake requires full agreement under the voluntary remake rule above.

For a regulation violation outside the mandatory early restart window, the innocent side may reject the remake request and report the match as **not played**. They may also continue playing the match if they agree that the violation does not affect the ongoing match.

In any restart case:

- If a second disconnect happens, any player who did not cause the troubles may refuse another restart. If restart is refused, report the match as **not played**.
- Players may report the player who caused the trouble.

<Aside type="note" title="Surrender vs Remake">
  If the applicable regulation separately allows surrender by disconnecting, that surrender rule is not treated as a match interruption or remake request.
</Aside>

<Aside type="caution" title="Disconnect without valid reason">
  If a player disconnects mid-match without a valid remake reason and the applicable regulation does not treat the leave as surrender, report the match as **not played**. If no reasonable explanation is provided for the leave, reporting the player is recommended.
</Aside>

### Post-match violation discovery

If regulation violation is discovered after the match ends, players cannot make a remake request.
Treat it as a problem about result validity.

- If the match thread is still open and no result has been accepted yet, do not approve a normal win or loss until the issue is resolved. Players should preserve evidence.
- If the innocent side agrees that the violation did not affect the match, players must accept the actual played result.
- If the innocent side decides the violation did affect the match, and if it is not challenged (see note below), report the match as **not played** with the violation reason.

<Aside type="note" title="NOTE: The innocent-side decision rule">
  The violating side may challenge the innocent side decision only with evidence that disproves it, and cannot claim **not played** when the innocent side is willing to accept the played result.
</Aside>
