## Arena, public matches, and tournaments

All three forms of play are valuable, but they answer different questions.

| | Public matches | Tournaments | DBD Arena |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary purpose | Accessible practice, variety, progression, and casual competition | Determine which complete team wins an event | Build repeated results for specific killers and persistent survivor groups, with the strongest emphasis on stable full parties |
| Match setting | A standalone match formed by official matchmaking | Part of a bounded event or series, often using mirrored games and team-level scoring | A standalone match under a persistent regulation, repeatable across opponents and over time |
| Competitor represented | Individual players under official matchmaking | The tournament team: killer player and survivor roster together | A killer player on a specific killer, or a persistent survivor group in that killer context |
| Information produced | A high volume of results with many uncontrolled variables | Strong short-term evidence about team performance in one event | Finer-grained evidence accumulated across repeatable matches over time |

Tournaments control many variables and are well suited to deciding who advances
or wins. Their team-level structure is less suited to separating a player's
performance on a particular killer from a survivor group's performance against
that killer.

Arena is designed around that separation and accumulation. This does not make
Arena more competitive than tournament play; it gives Arena a different job.

## Limits of the model

Arena ratings are estimates and matchmaking infrastructure, not a final answer
to who is best. They depend on the matches played, the active population, the
regulation, and the contexts the service records.

The regulations also shape what performance means. A result under one set of
maps, restrictions, and win conditions should not automatically be treated as
proof about a different format.

Arena does not replace public matches or tournaments. It is a community-run
service and is not official Dead by Daylight. Its results do not affect
in-game rank, grade, matchmaking rating, or progression. A public leaderboard
or ranked recognition system would be a separate product feature, not a
substitute for the underlying rating model.
