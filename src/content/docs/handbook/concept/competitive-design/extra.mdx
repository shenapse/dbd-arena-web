## Arena, public matches, and tournaments

All three forms of play are valuable, but they answer different questions.

| | Public matches | Tournaments | DBD Arena |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary purpose | Accessible practice, variety, progression, and casual play | Determine which complete team wins an event | Provide designed game environments and let players build repeated results |
| Match setting | A standalone match formed by official matchmaking | Part of a bounded event or series, often using mirrored games and team-level scoring | A standalone repeatable match under a pre-defined format, formed by Arena's matchmaking |
| Competitor represented | Individual players under official matchmaking | The tournament team: killer player and survivor roster together | Format-dependent: typically, killer player and persistent survivor group |
| Information produced | A high volume of results with many uncontrolled variables | Strong short-term evidence about team performance in one event | Finer-grained evidence accumulated across repeatable matches over time |

Tournaments control many variables and are well suited to deciding who advances
or wins. Their team-level structure is less suited to separating a killer player and survivor-team
performance from joint team result.

Arena is designed around that separation and accumulation. This does not make
Arena more competitive than tournament play; it gives Arena a different job.
