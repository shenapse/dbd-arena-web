## Defining the competitor unit

Arena rating is attached to a defined competitor inside a defined match context:

```text
(competitor, match context) -> rating
```

The [competitor-context rating model](/handbook/concept/competitive-design/competitor-context-rating-model/)
explains why Arena does not rate a player in general. This page focuses only on
the first half of that pair: how Arena decides what the competitor is.

A competitor unit is the player or group that a format treats as the owner of a
performance. It is the unit whose results can accumulate into a rating.

## The attribution principle

The competitor unit is defined by the competitive question behind the format.

Arena formats are pre-defined match environments. Each format is designed to
test a particular kind of performance. Once that question is defined, the
natural competitor unit is the unit whose performance answers it.

From a designer's perspective:

```text
competitive question -> natural competitor unit
```

From a player's perspective, this means the competitor unit is
format-dependent. Arena does not decide the competitor unit with one fixed rule
such as "always an individual player" or "always a team." The result is
attributed to the unit whose performance the format was designed to observe.

## Example: 1v1 symmetric

In a symmetric 1v1 format, the competitive question is:

**How good is this player in the standard role-alternating 1v1 format?**

Because both players play both roles inside the match structure, the match is
not asking only how good a player is as Killer or only how good a player is as
Survivor.

The natural competitor unit is therefore the player.

## Example: 1v4 quartet

In 1v4 quartet, the competitive question is:

```text
How good are this killer player and this survivor team in standard 1v4 competition with voice?
```

The format asks players to declare their side at queue time, so their in-match roles are already
fixed before the match is formed.

For the killer side, the obvious competitor unit is the killer player. The
killer player enters as the single player responsible for the killer-side
performance.

For the survivor side, the format assumes a prepared survivor team coordinating
at a high level with voice. The result is not cleanly evidence about four
independent solo survivor ratings. It is evidence about the team's shared
performance.

The natural survivor-side competitor unit is therefore the whole survivor team.

## What to take away

Arena does not use one universal competitor unit for every format. The format's
competitive question decides the unit.

In simple individual formats, the competitor can be the player. In coordinated
team formats, the competitor can be the team. Rating dimensions then define the
context in which that competitor's results accumulate.
