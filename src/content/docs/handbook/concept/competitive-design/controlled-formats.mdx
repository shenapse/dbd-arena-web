---
title: Controlled Formats
description: "A format defines match conditions that decide what kind of test the match is supposed to be."
---

Players explicitly apply to and play a pre-defined Arena format. The format is the published
competitive environment for the match: the frame that says what kind of test is
being played.

A controlled format does not make every match equal. It makes the important
conditions explicit enough that results from the same format can be treated as
comparable evidence.

## What a format controls

A controlled format defines the match conditions that make a result better
evidence for a specific competitive question. It decides what kind of test the
match is supposed to be.

For example, if the question is performance in a standard 1v4 (full party with
VC), the format treats survivors as a four-player party rather than four
unrelated individuals, and defines other match conditions assuming that context.
If the question depends on stable environmental conditions, the format can
restrict map pools, limit luck-dependent perks or actions, define loadout rules.

A format also decides what not to remove. Strategy ih gameplay including tunneling/camping, coordination with or without VC, mindgame, and situational judgment may all matter
to the competitive question. Those factors are part of the test. The format
should reduce noise that blurs interpretation without removing the skills or
decisions the match is meant to measure.

Concretely,  format may define things such as team structure, queue unit, allowed killers,
map selection, voice assumptions, pre-match reveals, loadout restrictions,
result facts, win/draw conditions, and whether the format is rated.

## Creating comparability

A controlled format contributes to enhanced comparability.

Results from the same format belong in the same conversation because they came
from the same stated competitive environment. A killer result in one format can
be compared more responsibly with other killer results from that format. A
survivor group's result can be compared more responsibly with results from the
same kind of survivor-group test.

This is not perfect equality. Two matches in the same format can still differ
in participants, opponent strength, random variation, and the details of how the
game unfolded. The format does not erase those differences. It gives the
system a stable basis for saying that the results are comparable enough to
accumulate together.

The opposite is also important. Results from different formats should not be
treated as interchangeable just because they share a surface outcome. A 4-kill
result, an escape, or a draw means less if the conditions that produced it are
not part of the record.

## Limits

A controlled format is not a claim that one match proves who is better. It is
not a promise that the format is perfectly balanced, and it is not a way to
remove all variation.

The format only answers a narrower design need: it makes the important match
conditions explicit, attaches the result to that declared context, and keeps
unlike results from being treated as the same kind of evidence.
