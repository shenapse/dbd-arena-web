DBD Arena organizes competitive Dead by Daylight custom games. Killers queue
alone with a chosen killer, while survivors queue as a team. Arena forms a match, gives both
sides the match conditions, and updates their ratings after the result is
confirmed.

It is a way to play serious, repeatable matches without waiting for a
tournament.

## What an Arena match looks like

The live service runs in Discord, and the match itself is played as a normal
custom game in Dead by Daylight.

1. **Join the queue.** A killer chooses an available killer character.
   Survivors queue together as a team.
2. **Get matched.** Arena looks for opponents whose relevant ratings are close
   enough to make the match competitive.
3. **Review the match.** Both sides see the participants, killer, map context,
   rules, and win conditions before accepting.
4. **Play the custom game.** The players create the lobby and play in Dead by
   Daylight.
5. **Confirm the result.** Both sides report and agree on what happened. The
   service records the result and updates the relevant Arena records.

That is the complete loop. Arena coordinates the competition; it does not
replace the game itself.

## Choose how you compete

### Queue as killer

Join alone and declare the killer you want to play. Your Arena record for that
killer is kept separate from your record on other killers, because playing
Nurse, Spirit, and Blight tests different skills.

This gives your practice a clear target: improve with a specific killer and
face survivor teams whose rating in that matchup is close enough to yours.

### Queue as survivor

Form a survivor team and join the queue together. Arena matches your team
against a killer whose relevant rating is close enough to provide a
competitive game.

Playing repeatedly with the same team builds a record for that lineup and
gives you a consistent way to measure performance and improvement together.

## What makes an Arena result different?

Public matches contain many unknowns. Arena makes several important conditions
explicit:

- Every match uses a published competitive format with known rules.
- Both sides know who is playing and which killer they will face.
- The format defines the map context, restrictions, and result conditions.
- Matchmaking considers the relevant ratings for the killer and survivor team.
- Accepted results build ratings across repeated matches over time.

These controls do not remove adaptation, mistakes, pressure, or clutch plays.
Those are still part of Dead by Daylight. They make it easier to understand
what a win or loss means and to prepare for the next match.

## What you can play now

**DBD 1v4 Quartet Ranked** is the active format. It offers coordinated
killer-versus-survivor-team matches. Nurse, Spirit, and Blight are currently
available in its killer pool.

The format has general rules and balancing for each available killer. Open the
[active format](/regulations/1v4-quartet/) to see its maps, restrictions,
win conditions, and full killer pool status.

## Arena, public matches, and tournaments

Each form of play has a different purpose.

| | Best suited for |
|---|---|
| Public matches | Accessible practice, variety, progression, and casual competition |
| Tournaments | Event stakes and deciding which complete team wins a bracket or series |
| DBD Arena | Repeatable, rated competition for specific killers and survivor teams |

Arena does not replace public matches or tournaments. It fills the space
between them: structured matches that can be played repeatedly and contribute
to an ongoing record.

## Interested in the design?

You do not need to understand the rating model or competitive theory before
playing. If you want to know why Arena identifies competitors this way, which
sources of uncertainty it controls, and what its results can and cannot show,
read [Competitive Design of DBD Arena](/handbook/concept/competitive-design/).

## Start playing

DBD Arena is a community-run service. It is not official Dead by Daylight, and
its ratings do not affect your in-game grade, matchmaking rating, or
progression.
