**Structured Dead by Daylight competition, built to be repeated.**

DBD Arena organizes rated custom matches for killers and survivor teams. Play
under published rules, face opponents selected using relevant Arena ratings,
and build evidence of your performance across matches, opponents, and time.

Each match stands on its own. You can compete seriously without entering a
tournament bracket or committing to a complete event.

## Competition built for repeated play

Public matches can be played repeatedly, but they are not organized around
fixed competitive conditions. Tournaments provide strict rules and direct
competition, but each event is bounded.

DBD Arena uses published rules and restrictions in standalone matches that can
be played again and again. Each accepted result contributes to a longer
competitive record, allowing evidence of performance to accumulate across
opponents and over time.

## Choose how you compete

### Queue as killer

Join alone and choose the killer you want to play. Arena looks for a survivor
team using your record with that killer, giving your practice a specific target
and your results a consistent context.

### Queue as a survivor team

Join the queue with other survivors and face a killer selected using the
relevant Arena records. Play repeatedly with your group to build a competitive
record together.

## What you can play now

**DBD 1v4 Quartet Ranked** is the active format. It provides rated,
killer-versus-survivor-team matches under one persistent competitive ruleset.
Nurse, Spirit, and Blight are currently available in its killer pool.

The format defines its maps, restrictions, win conditions, and balancing for
each available killer. Review the
[DBD 1v4 Quartet Ranked rules](/regulations/1v4-quartet/) to see the complete
match conditions.

## What an Arena match looks like

The live service coordinates matches in Discord. The match itself is played as
a normal custom game in Dead by Daylight.

1. **Join the queue.** Queue alone as a killer or together as a survivor team.
2. **Review and play the match.** Arena provides the participants, killer, map
   context, rules, and win conditions before both sides play in-game.
3. **Confirm the result.** Both sides report what happened. Once accepted, the
   result is recorded and the relevant ratings are updated.

Arena coordinates the competition; it does not replace the game itself. The
[Start Playing guide](/start/) explains what you need and how to join the live
service.

## Ready to compete?

DBD Arena is a community-run service. It is not official Dead by Daylight, and
its ratings do not affect your in-game grade, matchmaking rating, or
progression.

## Curious about the design?

You do not need to understand the rating model or competitive theory before
playing. If you want to know why Arena identifies competitors this way, which
sources of uncertainty it controls, and what its results can reasonably show,
read [Competitive Design of DBD Arena](/handbook/concept/competitive-design/).
